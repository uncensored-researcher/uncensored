My Faith: A Personal Journey Through Scripture

PERSONAL REFLECTION December 12, 2025 10 min read

Throughout my childhood, I was taught that God is a Trinity - three beings who are one. I accepted this because it was what I was told, although I struggled to explain it since it was described as a "mystery". I remember around the age of twelve, hearing my classmates discuss the triune God and briefly thinking to myself that the Holy Spirit might be angels.

Later at around sixteen, I encountered teachings similar to those of Jehovah's Witnesses, which presented Christ as a created being. For a moment, I wondered, but I was quickly convinced otherwise. Then in late 2024, someone close to me suggested the Holy Spirit is merely the words of God. That shook me but through reading, God guided me to truth. This is what I've found in Scripture.

An Aesthetic Visual depiction of God and Jesus

Who Is the Holy Spirit?

"And I will pray the Father, and he shall give you another Comforter, that he may abide with you for ever; Even the Spirit of truth; whom the world cannot receive, because it seeth him not, neither knoweth him: but ye know him; for he dwelleth with you, and shall be in you. I will not leave you comfortless: I will come to you." John 14:16-18

In these words, Jesus says the disciples already know the Spirit of truth because He dwells with them. Who did the disciples know at that time? Jesus Himself. So why does He call the Spirit "another Comforter"? Did Jesus ever refer to Himself in the third person? Yes. Look Here:

"These words spake Jesus, and lifted up his eyes to heaven, and said, Father, the hour is come; glorify thy Son, that thy Son also may glorify thee. As thou hast given Him power over all flesh, that He should give eternal life to as many as thou hast given Him." John 17:1-2

In Revelation, Jesus is depicted among the lampstands, speaking to the churches. [Revelation 1:13 "..And in the midst of the seven candlesticks one like unto the Son of man"] . Yet it is written:

"He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith unto the churches." Revelation 2:7

We know it was Jesus speaking so the Spirit speaking is Christ Himself. Paul affirms this:

"Now the Lord is that Spirit: and where the Spirit of the Lord is, there is liberty." 2 Corinthians 3:17

And again:

"And so it is written, The first man Adam was made a living soul; the last Adam was made a quickening spirit." 1 Corinthians 15:45

The "last Adam" is Jesus Christ, He became a life-giving Spirit. Now I do understand and also recognize that the Holy Spirit is the power of God (Acts 1:8), even as Jesus breathed on His disciples and said, "Receive ye the Holy Ghost" (John 20:22). The Holy Spirit is the presence and power of Christ, not a separate third person of the Godhead.

Who Is God/Jesus?

The bible reveals this clearly. John 3:16 lays the foundation:

"For God so loved the world, that He gave his only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life." John 3:16

The answer is in the verse - God is the Father of Jesus. Jesus is the Son of God. Proverbs 8 speaks of wisdom - which Paul identifies as Christ (1 Corinthians 1:24, 30). Jesus says:

"I was set up from everlasting, from the beginning, or ever the earth was. When there were no depths, I was brought forth; when there were no fountains abounding with water. Before the mountains were settled, before the hills was I brought forth." Proverbs 8:23-24

"Brought forth" means begotten or to come out from something, as the earth brings forth grass (Genesis 1:11-12 1 And God said, Let the earth bring forth grass - 12 And the earth brought forth grass). Ha Jesus ever spoken in such a manner? Yes He did! Jesus says:

"2For the Father himself loveth you, because ye have loved me, and have believed that I came out from God. I came forth from the Father, and am come into the world: again, I leave the world, and go to the Father " John 16:27-28

He was not created but He was begotten. Hebrews 1 explains clearly saying:

"God, who at sundry times and in divers manners spake in time past unto the fathers by the prophets, Hath in these last days spoken unto us by his Son, whom he hath appointed heir of all things, by whom also he made the worlds; Who being the brightness of his glory, and the express image of his person, and upholding all things by the word of his power, when he had by himself purged our sins, sat down on the right hand of the Majesty on high: Being made so much better than the angels, as he hath by inheritance obtained a more excellent name than they. For unto which of the angels said he at any time, Thou art my Son, this day have I begotten thee? And again, I will be to him a Father, and he shall be to me a Son? And again, when he bringeth in the firstbegotten INTO the world, he saith, And let all the angels of God worship him" Hebrews 1:2-5

Key terms: firstbegotten, heir, inheritance, express image. A lion can only beget a lion; God beget Jesus who is as much God in nature as God Himself is. They share the same divine nature. So is Jesus God? Yes, only when understood as the literal, begotten Son.

Scripture consistently emphasizes this Sonship:

Witness to Christ's Sonship

  • God The Father: "This is my beloved Son." Matthew 3:17
  • Jesus Christ: "I am the Son of God." John 10:36
  • Satan: "If thou be the Son of God.." Matthew 4:3
  • Fallen Angels: "What have we to do with thee, Jesus, thou Son of God?" Matthew 8:29
  • John The Baptist: "And I saw, and bare record that THIS IS THE SON OF GOD." John 1:34
  • Paul: "And straightway he preached Christ in the synagogues, THAT HE IS THE SON OF GOD." Acts 9:20
  • Peter: "And Simon Peter answered and said, Thou art the Christ, THE SON OF THE LIVING GOD." Matthew 16:16

The God of the bible is the Father – and its not only a title, but He is a literal Father. He is the Father of Jesus Christ.

Summary

Key Understandings

  • Who is God? - God is the Father of Jesus Christ. (John 20:17)
  • Who is Jesus Christ? - Jesus is the literal, begotten Son of God. (John 3:16)
  • What is the Holy Spirit? - The Holy Spirit is the presence and power of God and Christ. (Psalm 51:11; Romans 8:9-10)
